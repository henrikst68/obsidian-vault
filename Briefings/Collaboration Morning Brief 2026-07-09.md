# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Thursday, 9 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- The latest "What's New in Teams" update landed on the heels of InfoComm 2026 in Las Vegas, the largest professional AV show in North America, with new admin controls so organizations can have confidence that the right people and tools are participating in their discussions, via a new Teams admin policy designed to give organizations more visibility.
- Microsoft is rolling out workplace check-in via Wi-Fi for Microsoft Places and Teams, designed to help employees coordinate in-person work by keeping their workplace location up to date when they're in the office.
- Teams will soon automatically tag third-party bots in lobbies, allowing organizers to control whether they can join meetings, and Microsoft is separately rolling out a new Efficiency Mode for Microsoft Teams for systems with limited CPU and memory resources to improve app responsiveness.
- Admins should note that Microsoft Teams will remove the "Require verification by participants (CAPTCHA)" policy from PowerShell starting late July 2026, marking the second stage of CAPTCHA retirement as Microsoft moves to default-on bot detection.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- July's headline: Copilot Cowork just went generally available — the headline in a month with 40+ Microsoft 365 Copilot updates, bringing new models, image generation, browser use, the Customize page, and consumption billing.
- Model choice keeps expanding — Anthropic's Claude is now available in Copilot Chat, giving users more flexibility in how they work for complex analysis and document understanding, alongside GPT-5.5 Instant for low-latency everyday tasks.
- Governance is tightening: new watermarks can be added to videos and audio content, with a policy setting controlling your organization's ability to add a visual or audio watermark to AI-generated or altered content — useful for Henrik's compliance conversations.
- Heads-up: the toggle allowing users to revert to the old Copilot experience disappears soon — on July 15 for standard release, the toggle will be removed and the updated experience will be default on without the option for users to switch back.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- Agent 365 has launched with ready-to-deploy partner agents including Zoho, a comprehensive business productivity and CRM platform, bringing Zoho's business data and workflows into Microsoft Teams, plus Miro, Manus, and n8n agent factories now governed under Entra Agent IDs.
- Microsoft has introduced the Microsoft Marketplace, a unified web destination to find, try, and buy its full catalog of cloud solutions, AI apps, and agents.
- July's roadmap is dense: a high-density roadmap month with major updates across Purview, Teams, OneDrive/SharePoint, and Copilot, with the common theme being AI-driven automation, stronger data governance, and more intelligent collaboration experiences.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- A new channel survey shows shifting MTR vendor momentum: Logitech, Cisco, Neat, Yealink, Q-SYS, and Shure are gaining channel momentum, while HP Poly, Crestron, Barco, Jabra, and EPOS are losing ground, largely due to alignment with the Teams roadmap.
- A new Microsoft framework is reshaping procurement: the Microsoft Device Ecosystem Platform (MDEP) is about to change which devices your reseller recommends, and buyers are advised to add MDEP to your vendor evaluation criteria now, even if your next refresh is 18 months away.
- Microsoft is extending Teams Rooms capability so that Teams Rooms devices with Teams Rooms Pro licenses can attend webinars and structured meetings as attendees on Android, using chat, reactions, raise hand, captions, and participant roster.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- Cisco is sunsetting older OS support for Webex: starting with version 46.7 on July 7, 2026, Webex will officially end support for iOS 17 and iPadOS 17, and new users will no longer be able to download the app for those versions.
- Slack pushed a major agentic upgrade: Slackbot's Model Context Protocol (MCP) client is now generally available, built on the open MCP standard, connecting any app to Slackbot, backed by a partner ecosystem of more than 20 MCP apps, including Amplitude, Atlassian, Box, Canva, Docusign, Gamma, Linear, Miro, Notion, Replit, Webflow, and Zoom.
- Zoom is leaning into compliance and cross-platform AI: Zoom Meetings now adds transcript retention controls and Microsoft Purview sensitivity labels for meetings, while AI Companion answers now reach into Salesforce, Slack, and other tools where work actually lives.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- Flag the **15 July Copilot experience cutover** internally — users lose the ability to revert once the toggle is removed; prep a short FAQ for the helpdesk.
- Benchmark your MTR estate against the Blue Touch Paper vendor momentum shift (Poly/Crestron/Jabra losing ground) before your next hardware refresh cycle, and check MDEP compliance with your AV integrator.
- Monitor Agent 365 partner-agent rollout (Zoho, Miro, Manus, n8n) for relevance to Henrik's team workflows, and watch for further Slack MCP/Slackbot competitive moves given Teams' own agent push.
