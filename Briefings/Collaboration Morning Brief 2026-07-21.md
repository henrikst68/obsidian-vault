# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Tuesday, 21 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- Microsoft is completing the rollout of an in-meeting "Meeting AI" toggle, letting licensed organizers and presenters turn Meeting AI (Copilot, Facilitator, recap) on or off during live meetings, with rollout starting early July 2026; General Availability began mid-July and is expected to complete by end of July 2026, across Windows, macOS, mobile and web.
- Teams Rooms on Android is getting a modernized Gallery view: it will prioritize participants sending video by default, offer an equal audio/video participant layout, use consistent aspect ratios, minimize tile movement, and let users hide the room's self-preview, targeted for GA this month per the Microsoft 365 Roadmap.
- Recent Teams releases add meeting recording governance — meeting recordings now automatically inherit the meeting's sensitivity label when admins enable label inheritance, ensuring access controls and data handling rules carry forward to the MP4 recording — plus a new **Organization Trust Score** for apps/agents to automate admin security reviews.
- Service health remains stable: Teams was reported operational as of July 20, with only isolated user reports and no major incidents flagged in the last 24 hours.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- July's headline Copilot release: Microsoft shipped 40-plus updates to Copilot across its 365 suite in July 2026, headlined by Copilot Cowork reaching general availability, alongside interface redesigns, better document navigation, watermarking for AI content, and Anthropic's Claude model becoming available as an option within Copilot Chat.
- The Claude integration is notable for enterprise choice: it gives enterprise users real model choice — until now Copilot defaulted to Microsoft's own models, but teams can now switch between Microsoft's offerings and Claude for tasks like document analysis, research, and structured writing.
- Governance is tightening for custom agents: customers can now submit agents built in Agent Builder to the Agent Store under "Built by your org" after admin review and approval in the Microsoft 365 Admin Center, helping organizations share validated agents at scale while maintaining quality and governance.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- Pricing shift landed July 1: Microsoft introduced two new bundled offerings — Microsoft 365 Business Standard with Copilot and Microsoft 365 Business Premium with Copilot — giving Business-tier customers an integrated path to Copilot without managing separate subscriptions; partners were urged to lock in renewals before the change.
- SMB-focused "Copilot in 30" is coming to CSP: built for organizations with fewer than 300 employees, it combines a new 25-user, 30-day Microsoft 365 Copilot Business trial with partner resources, available in CSP starting August 1, 2026.
- Teams Rooms hardware management continues consolidating: administrators can now use the Pro Management portal to monitor device health, manage inventory, and access analytics for both Windows and Android devices, as management of Android-based Teams Rooms, panels, and phones moves from the Teams admin center to the Pro Management portal.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- Deployment guidance updated this week recommends, by room size: Poly Studio X70, Yealink MeetingBar A40 or Logitech Rally Bar for medium rooms; Crestron Flex, Poly G85-T or Neat Bar Pro for large rooms; and Logitech Sight + Rally Bar for signature panoramic rooms, with Teams Rooms Pro priced at $40/room/month, adding Pro Portal management, intelligent speaker, Whiteboard, front-row layout, and Copilot in Teams Rooms.
- BYOD peripheral support is expanding: room and desk peripheral support now includes docking stations and webcams, with IT admins benefiting from automatic device-to-space association, inventory, and detailed peripheral reports in the Pro Management portal.
- Certified hardware ecosystem remains broad, spanning Crestron, DTEN, EPOS, Jabra, Logitech, MaxHub, Neat, Poly, Shure, Yealink, AudioCodes and Cisco for Android-based Teams Rooms.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- Salesforce is pushing Slack deeper into sales workflows: as of July 16, Sales Cloud context and actions now live natively in Slack for sellers, with Salesforce positioning Agentforce Sales as the layer that unifies CRM data, AI, and approvals directly in channels, moving reps away from tab-hopping.
- Google rebranded its research tool: Google is renaming NotebookLM to Gemini Notebook, reflecting how it will evolve to do more across the Google ecosystem, with the new name and logo rolling out across interfaces over the next several weeks starting July 16.
- Google also expanded Gemini in Docs language support to 11 more languages including Mandarin, Dutch, Malay, Hebrew, Polish, Turkish, Czech, Indonesian, Swedish, Danish, and Norwegian, broadening its AI writing assistant's global reach.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- **Comms/policy check:** Confirm Teams "Meeting AI" toggle rollout has reached your tenant (GA completing end of July) and brief meeting organizers on the new on/off controls for Copilot, Facilitator and Recap.
- **Licensing review:** Assess renewal timing against the new Business Standard/Premium with Copilot bundled SKUs and pricing changes that took effect July 1.
- **Competitive watch:** Monitor Salesforce's Agentforce-in-Slack push (July 16) as a direct competitive counter to Teams+Copilot for sales/collaboration workflows — worth a comparison note for stakeholders evaluating platform strategy.
