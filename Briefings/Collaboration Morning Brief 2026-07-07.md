# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Tuesday, 7 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- **Possible service degradation today:** Microsoft Teams drew outage reports on Tuesday, July 7, 2026, after UptimeRobot detected unusual response times while other trackers continued to list Teams as operational and Microsoft had not publicly confirmed a platform-wide incident by early afternoon Eastern time. Treat as a "degraded for some" situation rather than a confirmed global outage.
- **Teams Phone multi-line now broadly rolling out:** Teams Phone user multi-line gives administrators the ability to assign up to 10 phone numbers to a single user, with full support across Teams desktop, mobile, and Teams devices.
- **Admin visibility improvements:** User-reported security signals are now surfaced in Teams admin center, letting admins view and download signals from messages users flag as security concerns to fine-tune policies.
- **Chat list reorganisation coming:** Rolling out mid-to-late July, new "Muted chats" and "Meeting chats" sections will automatically group conversations to keep the chat list organised.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- **Copilot Cowork hits full GA:** Cowork is now generally available to all Microsoft 365 Copilot tenants, bringing new models, image generation, browser use, a Customize page, and consumption billing.
- **Model choice expands:** Claude Opus 4.7 is now available in Microsoft 365 Copilot, helping complete complex, multi-step work with stronger instruction-following and higher-resolution visual understanding.
- **AI-content transparency:** Microsoft is enabling watermarks for AI-generated video and audio content, controlled via a policy setting that adds visual or audio watermarks to content users generate or alter with AI.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- **July 1 pricing/packaging shakeup now in effect:** Copilot is no longer a promotional add-on — it becomes a permanent part of the Microsoft 365 lineup, with the separate Copilot add-on folding into new bundled Business plans.
- **Agent 365 licensing tightens:** Effective June 1, Microsoft introduced expanded license prerequisites for new Agent 365 purchases, though Microsoft 365 E7 customers are unaffected since E7 already includes E5, Agent 365, Copilot, and Entra Suite.
- **Teams Rooms Pro gets a design tool:** A new Teams Room builder in the Pro Management portal helps IT managers visually design and configure Teams Rooms across traditional, signature, and flex spaces, simplifying device and license selection.

## 🖥️ Meeting Room Equipment
- **Vendor momentum is shifting:** Logitech, Cisco, Neat, Yealink, Q-SYS, and Shure are gaining channel momentum, while HP Poly, Crestron, Barco, Jabra, and EPOS are losing ground, according to channel partner sentiment.
- **Reasoning behind the split:** Growing vendors align tightly with Teams, deploy easily at scale, and carry strong commercial terms, while declining vendors struggle with slower innovation and weaker native fit with the Teams ecosystem.
- **Legacy infrastructure risk:** With the upcoming deprecation of Exchange Web Services in Exchange Online, Teams Phone devices must be updated to a newer app version to ensure room calendars continue to function.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- **Salesforce bundles Slack by default:** From June 15, any new Salesforce org in Enterprise or Unlimited edition comes with a Slack workspace already built and configured — no separate purchase or setup required. Microsoft's Teams still holds a systems-level advantage via its deep integration with Windows, Office, and Azure, so this is a competitive signal worth watching rather than an immediate threat.
- **Zoom deepens cross-app AI reach:** Zoom's AI Companion now reaches into Salesforce, Slack, and other connected tools when answering questions, rather than stopping at Zoom data alone.
- **Google Workspace pushes Gemini further into core apps:** Google is bringing the "Ask Gemini in Drive" feature — previously web-only — to the Drive Android and iOS apps.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- **Monitor today's Teams status closely** — reports are ambiguous/regional rather than confirmed; check the admin Service Health Dashboard before escalating internally.
- **Review your renewal dates against the July 1 Microsoft 365 pricing/packaging change** — Copilot is now bundled by default in Business Standard/Premium, and standalone Copilot pricing has risen from promo to standard rate.
- **Revisit meeting room hardware roadmap** — if your estate leans on Poly, Crestron, or Jabra, factor in the channel's diverging momentum toward Logitech/Yealink/Neat when planning next refresh cycle.
