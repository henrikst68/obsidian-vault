# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Friday, 17 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- Microsoft Teams will support breakout rooms in meetings with up to 1,000 attendees, allowing up to 200 breakout rooms per meeting, rolling out globally in July 2026.
- Teams Rooms on Android is set to gain a modernized Gallery view prioritising participants sending video, with General Availability targeted for July 2026 (still marked "in development").
- Security watch: initial access broker KongTuke has moved to Microsoft Teams for social engineering attacks, taking as little as five minutes to gain persistent access to corporate networks. Separately, DragonForce ransomware used a custom malware named 'Backdoor.Turn' to hide command-and-control traffic inside Microsoft Teams relay infrastructure.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- Copilot Cowork, an agentic system that plans, executes, and delivers real work, is now generally available worldwide — the headline Copilot release this month.
- Anthropic's Claude is now available in Copilot Chat, giving users more flexibility, with users able to select it as a model option for complex analysis, document understanding, and structured content generation.
- Microsoft is rolling out watermarking for AI-generated video and audio content across Microsoft 365 to increase transparency about AI-altered content.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- Starting July 1, 2026, Microsoft 365 Business Standard with Copilot and Business Premium with Copilot become permanent SKUs, removing the friction of selling Copilot as a short-term promotional add-on for SMBs.
- Teams meetings can now be linked to existing Microsoft Planner plans instead of auto-creating separate plans, improving task tracking continuity across ongoing initiatives.
- Admins can now approve and publish org-built Copilot agents from Agent Builder into the Agent Store, enabling governed discovery and reuse at scale.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- Neat has launched "Neat Center," a 360° companion device that lets remote attendees better hear and see everyone in the room, even when in-room participants are facing away from the main device.
