# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Tuesday, 14 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- Microsoft is rolling out a new in-meeting "Meeting AI" toggle letting organizers and presenters turn Copilot, Facilitator and Recap on or off during live meetings, with rollout beginning with Targeted Release in early July 2026 and General Availability starting mid-July, completing by end of July.
- A batch of smaller Teams updates is landing this month, including near-complete rollout of apps in private channels, a new Speaker-focused layout for Teams events, and a Quick Share option for images across channels and chats — though Microsoft has paused rollout of a minimized-meeting update after user pushback, needing more time to fix bugs.
- Admins should note two release-note items from early July: meeting recordings can now automatically inherit the meeting's sensitivity label when admins enable label inheritance, alongside streamlined Teams Phone call transfers.
- Security teams should be aware that threat actor UNC6692 has been using Microsoft Teams helpdesk impersonation to deploy the new SNOW malware suite, and a macOS screen-sharing bug is causing blank screens for some users.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- Teams' controversial **Facilitator** feature — which watches or listens to meetings and proactively shares web-search answers when it detects a knowledge gap — is disabled by default and rolling out from early August 2026 for Targeted Release, reaching general availability by late August.
- Microsoft is diversifying its model stack: Microsoft is using more of its own MAI models in Copilot scenarios for efficiency, alongside Anthropic and OpenAI, with "Auto" as the default so Microsoft can swap models as needed.
- Governance is advancing via Microsoft 365 E7 and Agent 365, now generally available, delivering a unified foundation for secure AI and agent governance at scale, plus AI-generated content watermarking now configurable via policy.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- Slack's **Slackbot MCP client** is now generally available, connecting apps and shared channels through one conversational workspace, with a partner ecosystem of 20+ apps including Salesforce, Canva, Linear, and Zoom.
- Cisco shipped Webex App 46.7 on 9 July, and is ending support for iOS 17 and iPadOS 17 with this release — a housekeeping item for admins managing mobile fleets.
- Regional telephony expansion continues: Digicel and LoopUp have partnered to bring Microsoft Teams telephony to the Caribbean region.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- Microsoft's **Express Install** "room-in-a-box" approach is expanding via OEM partners, letting organizations scale quickly to fully AI-enabled Teams Rooms with a cost-effective, faster deployment path, complementing reports of 30–45% cost savings and ~1-hour installs.
- Channel intelligence shows Microsoft Teams dominating hardware decisions, with platform certification now a dealbreaker for enterprise buyers, cloud video interoperability fading, and a new Microsoft Device Ecosystem Platform (MDEP) set to reshape device recommendations.
- On the vendor front, Surface Hub 3 continues rolling out an AI layer with IntelliFrame tracking and Facilitator note-taking/summarizing features, while Neat and Barco are previewing intelligent-framing and BYOD hub capabilities for later in H2 2026.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- Zoom continues deepening its AI Companion's cross-app reach, letting users pull data from up to 10 third-party apps — including Slack, Salesforce, Workday, and Seismic — directly into AI Companion queries, reaching into tools where work actually lives.
- Gartner has shifted Slack into its "Enterprise AI Assistants" category alongside Zoom, Webex, Teams and Google Workspace, noting a continued industry effort to move from AI-enabled assistants to fully agentic AI that acts autonomously on a user's behalf.
- Analysts frame this as a platform land-grab: vendors are locked in a continued battle to be "the top of the stack" — the place where users start their day, reminiscent of earlier browser/OS bundling wars.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- Review your tenant's **Meeting AI toggle** rollout timeline (mid-to-late July GA) and decide default policy before end-user complaints arise.
- Check whether **sensitivity label inheritance** for meeting recordings is enabled — a compliance gap could otherwise open up for confidential recordings.
- Monitor the **UNC6692 Teams helpdesk impersonation** campaign and brief security teams; consider enabling Teams' new bot/impersonation warning features proactively.
