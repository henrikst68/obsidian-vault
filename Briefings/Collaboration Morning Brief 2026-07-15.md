# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Wednesday, 15 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- **Service disruption (July 14):** Microsoft 365 was reportedly down for hundreds of users on July 14, 2026, with report volume surging around 2:30PM Eastern Time, echoing an earlier incident where Microsoft tagged an ongoing outage affecting Office Apps and Teams file access as an incident tied to a cache configuration change that required a failover.
- **macOS screen-sharing bug:** A newly reported issue is causing blank screens during screen sharing on Teams for macOS (Cyber Security News, 11 July) — worth flagging to Mac-heavy teams.
- **Bot lobby protections rolling out:** Microsoft has introduced a new Teams admin policy that allows organizers to prevent third-party bots from joining meetings without approval — when enabled, Teams automatically detects potential bots, places them in the lobby, clearly identifies them, and prompts organizers to confirm admission.
- **Telephony expansion:** Digicel and LoopUp have partnered to bring Microsoft Teams telephony to the Caribbean region (Total Telecom, 9 July).

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- **Model choice expands:** Anthropic's Claude is now available in Copilot Chat, giving users more flexibility in how they work, letting them select Claude as a model option for complex analysis, document understanding, and structured content generation; separately, Claude Opus 4.7 brings stronger instruction following, improved tool selection, and higher resolution visual understanding to Copilot's model roster.
- **Interface overhaul incoming:** Microsoft's July Copilot update introduces a redesigned layout with revised menus and navigation, reorganized frequently-used features, and a new Tasks tab to monitor activities in progress, alongside a refreshed chat conversation format.
- **Governance feature:** Watermarks for AI-generated content are rolling out across Copilot, with a policy setting controlling whether organizations can add visual or audio watermarks to AI-generated video and audio — relevant for compliance teams.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- **SharePoint/Viva consolidation continues:** SharePoint home site updates are rolling out following the rename of Viva Connections to the SharePoint app in Teams, with SharePoint home site technology now fully powering the Connections experience.
- **Marketplace integration:** AnywhereNow has brought its Tendfor solution to the Microsoft Marketplace (Call Centre Helper, 8 July), part of the broader push toward Microsoft's unified app marketplace.
- **Power Platform AI tooling:** The Canvas Apps MCP Authoring Plugin now lets makers use AI coding agents like GitHub Copilot or Claude Code to build canvas apps through natural conversation, running locally alongside a live Power Apps Studio session.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- Microsoft's certified device ecosystem continues to lean on core partners — Microsoft is partnering and actively working with Yealink, Crestron, Polycom, and Audiocodes to develop and certify a wide portfolio of desk phones and conference room audio devices.
- Cisco continues to position its devices as multi-platform, noting it can run native Microsoft Teams Rooms on Microsoft Teams Rooms and Teams Panel-certified Cisco devices covering small to large workspaces, with rich interoperability including full-featured Webex Meetings.
- Yealink remains a leading Teams-certified hardware vendor, offering an expanding lineup of MeetingBars, touch consoles, and conference cameras for Teams and Zoom environments.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- **Slack goes default at Salesforce:** Per UC Today, Salesforce's Summer '26 release makes Slack the default collaboration tool for every new customer — a significant enterprise bundling move.
- **Slack ships MCP-powered Slackbot:** Slack has added the Slackbot MCP client, now generally available, connecting apps and shared channels through one conversational workspace, with a partner ecosystem of 20+ apps letting teams search, act, and share live results from tools like Salesforce, Canva, Linear, and Zoom.
- **Zoom deepens cross-platform AI:** Zoom's AI Companion can now take notes across third-party meeting platforms like Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and WebEx, intensifying competition for the "AI meeting layer" regardless of which video tool is used.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- **Monitor the July 14 Microsoft 365/Teams disruption** for a formal post-incident report — confirm no lingering file-access issues in your tenant.
- **Review the new Teams bot-lobby policy** in the admin center and decide whether to enforce approval requirements for third-party meeting bots, especially given rising social-engineering abuse of Teams (SNOW malware, helpdesk impersonation campaigns).
- **Track the Copilot interface redesign (new Tasks tab) rollout** this month and prepare a short user-comms note ahead of it landing in Henrik's tenant.
