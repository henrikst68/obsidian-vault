# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Thursday, 7 May 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams

- **Browser compatibility deadline approaching:** From 15 May 2026, Teams on the web will require supported browser versions — worth verifying your organisation's browser policies before the cutoff.
- **Intelligent Recap Sharing coming in June:** AI-generated meeting recaps will soon be shareable directly from Teams, but will require a Teams Premium or Copilot licence.
- **UI and performance updates rolling out:** Teams is getting a redesigned toolbar (users can pin, unpin, and reorder controls) plus a new Efficiency Mode that reduces resource consumption on lower-spec devices.
- **Teams Shared Space pricing change:** Effective 1 April 2026, Teams Shared Space – Single Space products are now priced at $0; standard Shared Space licences remain at $8/user.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams

- **Agent 365 and M365 E7 went GA on 1 May:** Microsoft's new top-tier SKU bundles M365 E5, Copilot, Entra Suite, and the new Agent 365 platform — positioning AI agents as the core operating layer for enterprise work.
- **Copilot can now screen incoming Teams calls:** Rolling out in May, Copilot can answer calls, gather context from callers, and even schedule follow-ups via Microsoft Bookings — a significant step toward agentic telephony.
- **AI agent adoption surging:** Microsoft's 2026 Work Trend Index (released 5 May) reports 78% of knowledge workers use AI agents weekly, up from just 12% in 2024. Copilot Chat is now accessible across Teams chats, channels, calling, and meetings.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations

- **"Teamwork Deployment" specialisation renamed:** From 29 April, Microsoft rebranded it as the **Secure AI Productivity** specialisation, signalling a shift in partner focus toward AI governance and secure adoption.
- **Frontier Partner specialisation introduced:** Microsoft is evolving its Frontier Badge into a formal partner specialisation for those who build or deliver agents — part of a broader push announced 21 April.
- **Microsoft Marketplace hits 5,000+ AI solutions:** Partner-built agents and plugins are now searchable and purchasable through familiar enterprise procurement channels, lowering the barrier to Teams app discovery and deployment.

## 🖥️ Meeting Room Equipment

- **Shure IntelliMix Room Kits now shipping:** Certified for Microsoft Teams Rooms, the kits cover small, medium, and large spaces with class-leading audio/video and enterprise-ready security. Shure was also a Sponsoring Show Partner at ISE 2026 in Barcelona (February).
- **Top MTR vendors remain stable:** Logitech, Crestron, Poly (HP), Yealink, and Lenovo continue to lead the certified MTR hardware market; no major new entrants or discontinuations reported this week.
- **No breaking MTR hardware news in the last 48 hours** — steady-state for certified device rollouts.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape

- **Slack's Agentforce 360 maturing:** Launched in January 2026, the AI agent (available on Business+ and Enterprise+) is evolving Slackbot from a personal assistant into a full enterprise "AI teammate" capable of multi-step agentic tasks.
- **Zoom continues platform expansion:** Zoom Workplace now bundles Team Chat, Whiteboard, async Clips, Notes, and AI Companion — cementing its pivot from a video tool to a broader collaboration suite competing directly with Teams.
- **AI meeting features now table stakes:** Real-time transcription, auto-summaries, and action-item extraction are no longer premium differentiators — all major platforms (Teams, Zoom, Google Workspace, Webex) now include them at standard tiers.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch

- **🔴 15 May deadline:** Audit your organisation's browser versions for Teams web compatibility — users on unsupported browsers will lose access in 8 days.
- **📦 Review Agent 365 / M365 E7:** Now GA, these new SKUs may affect upcoming renewal conversations with customers or your own tenant planning.
- **📞 Test Copilot call-screening:** The incoming-call Copilot feature is rolling out this month — worth piloting in a controlled group to assess impact on call-handling workflows.
