# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Monday, 20 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- Microsoft has rolled out an **Organization Trust Score** for Teams apps and agents, replacing manual admin review with an automated evaluation process where admins define trust requirements once and the system generates a Trust Score and detailed evaluation report for each app and agent.
- **Meeting recordings now inherit sensitivity labels** automatically — when admins enable label inheritance, any labeled meeting applies the same label to its MP4 recording, and Copilot/agent responses based on transcripts reflect the meeting's sensitivity end to end.
- Teams users reported a service disruption on **14 July**, with outage reports surging around 2:45PM Eastern Time per DownDetector tracking — the second such gray-area incident this month after a similar 7 July event.
- Copilot-powered natural language search is expanding in Teams, letting users find chats, files, and other content by asking questions in natural language instead of searching exact keywords.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- **Anthropic's Claude** is now selectable inside Copilot Chat — users can select Claude as a model option in Copilot Chat for complex analysis, document understanding, and structured content generation.
- Microsoft is opening its **Agent Store** to org-built agents: customers can submit agents built in Agent Builder to the Agent Store's "Built by your org" section after admin review, enabling organizations to share validated agents at scale while maintaining quality and governance.
- Reports indicate Microsoft is using more of its own MAI models in M365 Copilot scenarios for efficiency on non-consumption workloads, adding model resilience alongside Anthropic and OpenAI, with "Auto" model selection giving Microsoft flexibility to swap models behind the scenes.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- **Cisco** shipped its first MDEP-powered MTR-certified device: at Cisco Live US 2026, the company introduced the Cisco Board Pro G3 — its first MTR Certified board powered by the Microsoft Device Ecosystem Platform, purpose-built for the agentic workplace.
- **HP** is deepening its device partnership, planning to integrate the Microsoft Device Ecosystem Platform into Poly video conferencing solutions, IP phones, room controllers, and scheduling displays for more consistent Teams experiences.
- **Barco ClickShare Hub** continues to expand as a wireless BYOD option for Teams Rooms, per recent Teams Insider podcast coverage.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- **Cisco** now offers MDEP-based MTR hardware across its line: the Desk Pro G2, Room Kit Pro G2, and new Board Pro G3, all built with the ability to run the Microsoft Device Ecosystem Platform for the Microsoft Teams Rooms experience.
- Microsoft reports the Teams Rooms install base has surpassed 1.5 million+ active rooms, with new OEM devices continuing to enter certification.
- Adoption resources highlight **Express Install** as a fast deployment path — a standardised deployment option for Teams Rooms that removes the need for custom modifications, letting rooms install in ~1 hour with 30–45% cost savings.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- **Slack** made its **Slackbot MCP client** generally available, connecting apps and shared channels through one conversational workspace, with a partner ecosystem of 20+ apps letting teams search, act, and share live results from tools like Salesforce, Canva, Linear, and Zoom directly in Slack.
- Salesforce's Summer '26 release reportedly makes **Slack the default collaboration layer for every new Salesforce customer**, signalling deeper CRM-chat bundling as a competitive play against Teams.
- **Zoom's AI Companion** is pushing further into cross-app territory, now able to pull data from up to 10 third-party apps — including Slack, Salesforce, Workday, and Seismic — directly into AI Companion queries, reaching into the tools where work actually lives rather than stopping at Zoom.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- **Governance:** Review whether to pilot the new Teams Organization Trust Score for third-party app/agent vetting — useful ammunition for security/compliance conversations.
- **Reliability:** Two partial Teams outages this month (7 July, 14 July) — worth a quick check with IT on whether monitoring/escalation playbooks need updating.
- **Competitive watch:** Slack's MCP-based agent ecosystem and Salesforce's "Slack by default" bundling are worth tracking as a growing challenge to Teams' collaboration-hub positioning, especially in sales-adjacent teams.
