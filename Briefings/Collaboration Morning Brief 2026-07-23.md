# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Thursday, 23 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- Users are reporting an ongoing calendar bug where both web and desktop Teams can no longer save new meetings in the calendar, though existing meetings can still be edited and impromptu meetings can still be started, with reports dating back to July 20.
- Microsoft continues rolling out a new Efficiency Mode for Microsoft Teams for systems with limited CPU and memory resources to improve app responsiveness.
- Security teams should note that initial access broker KongTuke has moved to Microsoft Teams for social engineering attacks, taking as little as five minutes to gain persistent access to corporate networks.
- Microsoft also shipped smarter anti-bot protections for Teams meetings following recent Patch Tuesday fixes, per BleepingComputer's rolling Teams coverage.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- Copilot Cowork has now gone generally available, described as the headline release among 40+ Microsoft 365 Copilot updates shipped this month.
- Admins can now submit agents built in Agent Builder to the Agent Store under the "Built by your org" section after admin review and approval in the Microsoft 365 Admin Center, enabling organizations to share validated agents at scale while maintaining quality and governance.
- Copilot in SharePoint is now on by default for all licensed users, with new capability letting people ask Copilot to create a Word document, Excel workbook, or PowerPoint deck directly from SharePoint content — turning existing knowledge into finished deliverables in minutes.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- The new Microsoft 365 Business with Copilot SKUs, previously promotional, are now generally available as of July 1, 2026, turning a successful promotional motion into a permanent, predictable offer for SMB customers.
- Microsoft's Copilot partner specialization has been renamed and restructured: starting July 2026, Microsoft renames the specialization to Microsoft 365 Copilot specialization, shifting the performance requirement to account-only for paid monthly active usage growth.
- Business-tier M365 plans are also gaining 50 GB of additional mailbox storage and enhanced Copilot Chat experiences, while E3 licenses now include Defender for Office 365 Plan 1.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- Logitech unveiled an AI-native meeting room platform with on-device NPUs (codenamed "Sage") for real-time transcription and noise suppression without cloud dependency, directly challenging Zoom and Microsoft Teams' cloud-first architectures.
- Neat and Logitech have pushed further into Google Meet territory, with the Neat Board series (32, 50, and Pro) and the Logitech Rally Board 65 now certified for Google Meet as the first Android-based all-in-one devices — a reminder that MTR hardware vendors are increasingly multi-platform.
- Vendor roundups continue to rank Logitech, Poly, Cisco, Yealink, Jabra, Crestron, Neat, HP and Dell as the leading room-hardware players for 2026 hybrid deployments.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- Salesforce has tightened its Slack/Agentforce play: as of July 16, Sales Cloud context and actions now live natively in Slack for sellers, with Salesforce positioning Agentforce Sales as the layer that unifies CRM data, AI, and approvals directly in channels.
- Zoom has quietly retired the "AI Companion" branding, confirming that the AI Companion name has been retired, though features like Meeting Summaries, Chat Compose, Scheduling Assistance, and Smart Recording will remain available throughout Zoom Workplace under a simplified "AI" settings menu.
- Zoom is also preparing general availability of real-time voice translation, letting participants project their translated speech back to other participants in the meeting, with listeners not needing the Voice Translator add-on to hear it, pending a backend update slated for July 27.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- Track the Teams "can't save new meetings" calendar bug — if it's hitting your org, expect an official Microsoft 365 status advisory soon; consider a workaround/communication to affected users in the interim.
- Watch for Copilot Agent Store governance rollout in your tenant — worth reviewing admin approval workflows before agents start proliferating.
- Keep an eye on Zoom's translator GA (July 27) and Logitech's NPU-based room hardware — both signal a broader shift toward on-device AI processing in meeting equipment that may affect future MTR procurement decisions.
