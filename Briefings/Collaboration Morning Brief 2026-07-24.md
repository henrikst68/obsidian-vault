# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Friday, 24 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- **Major M365 outage (July 23):** Microsoft was impacted by a massive outage affecting Teams and Microsoft 365 services, primarily in North America, with SharePoint accounting for 78% of complaints, followed by Excel at 11% and the Admin Center at 6%. The disruption was tied to a broader Azure incident, with intermittent connectivity failures and increased latency affecting the West US region; the outage was confirmed resolved by 9:55 PM ET the same day.
- Separately, initial access broker KongTuke has moved to Microsoft Teams for social engineering attacks, taking as little as five minutes to gain persistent access to corporate networks — worth flagging to security teams.
- On the roadmap front, Microsoft is extending Teams Phone's multi-line calling experience to iOS and Android, entering rollout for worldwide standard multi-tenant customers in July 2026 with line-aware call notifications and unified call history.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- Microsoft 365 Copilot can now help answer incoming Teams calls and schedule follow-up appointments via Copilot Call Delegation, part of a wider push to embed agentic AI into calling.
- Teams has added an Organization Trust Score for apps and agents, plus simplified external collaboration controls in the Admin Center — a governance-relevant update for IT.
- Model strategy shift: Bloomberg reports Microsoft is using more of its own MAI models in M365 Copilot scenarios, adding model resilience alongside Anthropic and OpenAI, explaining why "Auto" is now the default in many Copilot experiences.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- **Teams Rooms hardware tools:** a new Teams Room Builder in the Pro Management portal lets IT managers visually design and configure traditional, signature, and flex meeting spaces, speeding up rollout with simplified device and license selection.
- **Accessibility default change:** Microsoft is switching the live captions profanity filter from On to Off by default across Teams meetings, calls, events, and MTR devices to improve accessibility and align with EU requirements.
- **Salesforce/Slack shakeup:** from June 15, every new Salesforce Enterprise and Unlimited org ships with a fully provisioned Slack workspace, and Chatter is now turned off by default in new orgs — a notable competitive move against Teams in the CRM-adjacent collaboration space.

## Meeting Room Equipment
- Logitech, partnering with Urben Express, Vison Express and Samsung, is simplifying room installations with Express Install bundles for huddle, medium, and large Teams Rooms, deployable in under an hour with minimal labor.
- Teams Rooms on Android is getting a modernized Gallery view that prioritizes participants sending video, offers equal audio/video layouts, and lets users hide the self-preview, targeted for GA in July 2026 (still "in development").
- Neat is previewing an Intelligent Framing feature where the camera follows the conversation in larger rooms, expected in beta later this year.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- **Zoom:** continues expanding its AI Companion side panel to be product-specific (Mail, Calendar, Chat, Canvas, Phone) rather than a single global icon, and recently agreed to acquire Common Room, an AI-native go-to-market intelligence platform, to turn fragmented buyer signals into activated AI-agent insights.
- **Google Workspace:** Gemini Omni is now available directly within Google Vids, letting users create videos using a personal avatar, alongside GCPW support for FIDO2-compliant physical security keys for stronger admin login security.
- **Salesforce/Slack:** the Summer '26 push (above) signals Slack positioning itself as the default enterprise workflow layer for CRM-heavy organizations — a direct competitive angle against Teams for customer-facing collaboration.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- Confirm no lingering tenant impact from yesterday's SharePoint/Teams/Azure outage (incident MO1437424) before making any tenant-level admin changes today.
- Review exposure to Teams-based social engineering (KongTuke, external user impersonation) and consider tightening external collaboration/Trust Score settings in Teams Admin Center.
- Track the Salesforce Summer '26 Slack-by-default rollout if Henrik's org uses Salesforce — may introduce a shadow Slack footprint alongside Teams that needs a governance policy.
