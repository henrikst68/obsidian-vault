# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Monday, 13 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- Microsoft paused the general-availability rollout of new **minimized meeting views** (Expanded/Compact) in a July 7 Message Center update, with Microsoft’s own Message Center update stating tersely, "Updated July 7, 2026: We have paused release to GA at this time" — no reason was given.
- Teams drew **intermittent outage chatter** on July 7 after UptimeRobot detected unusual response times while other third-party trackers continued to list Teams as operational and Microsoft had not publicly confirmed a platform-wide incident; StatusGator shows Teams operational as of July 13, with 2 user-submitted outage reports in the past 24 hours.
- Admins are getting new controls: an **Organization Trust Score** for apps/agents that gives IT admins a scalable, automated evaluation process replacing manual trust review, generating a Trust Score and evaluation report, plus automatic blocking of unidentified external bots from meetings.
- **Teams Phone user multi-line** is now GA, allowing administrators to assign up to 10 phone numbers to a single user across desktop, mobile and Teams devices.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- Bloomberg reporting (via industry roundup) indicates Microsoft is using more of its own MAI models in M365 Copilot scenarios for efficiency on non-consumption workloads, adding model resilience alongside Anthropic and OpenAI, with "Auto" as the default model selector.
- **Anthropic's Claude** is now selectable in Copilot Chat, giving users more flexibility in how they work, with Claude available as a model option for complex analysis, document understanding, and structured content generation.
- Governance push continues: Microsoft added **AI content watermarking** for Copilot-generated video/audio and is rolling out **pay-as-you-go billing for Work IQ APIs**, giving organisations more flexible consumption of productivity-intelligence capabilities.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- **Teams Rooms Express Install** continues to standardise MTR deployment — a standardised deployment option that removes the need for custom modifications or specialist AV installation, allowing rooms to be installed in ~1 hour with 30–45% cost savings.
- **Planner Agent** is heading to GA with custom templates and fields, letting licensed users create, update, and manage personal tasks and shared basic plans directly inside Copilot with no context switching back to Planner.
- July's roadmap brings a broader **SharePoint/OneDrive UX refresh** (new Libraries view, Markdown co-authoring) alongside expanded Teams intelligence and control features including interpreter usage dashboards, automatic bot blocking, and call queue auto-recording.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- **Barco ClickShare Hub** and Logitech bundles have achieved Teams Rooms certification, part of a wider push to expand ClickShare from a best-in-class BYOD solution into fully integrated room systems.
- **Yealink** launched a Dual MTower centre-view intelligent camera for large rooms, while **Neat** is previewing an Intelligent Framing camera feature for bigger spaces in H2 2026.
- Certified MTR hardware remains concentrated among Logitech, Poly, HP, Yealink, Neat, Crestron, Cisco, Lenovo, Jabra, Sennheiser and Shure, with device firmware updates continuing to add Copilot Call Delegation support on Poly/Yealink/AudioCodes phones.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- **Slack** shipped its July update adding IDP groups visibility and made the **Slackbot MCP client** generally available, connecting apps and shared channels through one conversational workspace with a partner ecosystem of 20+ apps including Salesforce, Canva, Linear, and Zoom.
- **Webex** is ending support for iOS 17/iPadOS 17 starting with version 46.7 (July 7 release), with existing users able to continue on that version until February 2, 2027.
- **Zoom** continues cross-platform AI expansion — AI Companion's "My notes" feature works across third-party meeting platforms like Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, and WebEx, underscoring the trend of vendors chasing meetings regardless of host platform.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- Confirm whether your tenant enabled **sensitivity label inheritance** for Teams meeting recordings — labelled MP4s now carry the meeting's classification automatically, closing a compliance gap for confidential recordings.
- Watch for the **minimized meeting views** pause to be resolved or reversed; if user communications already went out about this feature, hold off further messaging until Microsoft clarifies.
- Track the July 7 Teams "signal noise" pattern (ambiguous outage reports) — worth flagging to IT ops as a reminder to trust official Service Health Dashboard over third-party trackers during ambiguous incidents.
