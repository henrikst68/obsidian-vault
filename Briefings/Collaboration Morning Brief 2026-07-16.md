# 🗓️ Collaboration Morning Brief — Thursday, 16 July 2026

## 🔵 Microsoft Teams
- **Reported outage/slowdown spike (15 July):** Microsoft Teams was reportedly down for hundreds of users on July 15, 2026, with Downdetector showing a surge in reports around 1:20 PM Eastern. Worth checking the admin health dashboard if your org saw disruptions yesterday afternoon.
- **Room optimization mode relocated:** General availability of a relocated, context-dependent "Room optimization mode" option begins mid-July 2026 and completes by late July; when enabled, Teams will optimize the meeting experience for room scenarios. Connected room peripherals will be automatically selected instead of laptop devices, and voice isolation will be disabled so in-room participants can be heard clearly.
- **Teams Rooms Android Gallery refresh:** Teams Rooms on Android will gain a modernized Gallery view that prioritizes video-first participants with an equal audio/video layout option, targeting General Availability in July 2026. Still listed as "in development" so treat as a rollout target rather than confirmed availability.
- **Bot visibility in meeting lobbies:** Organizers will now be able to see a clear representation of external third-party bots waiting in the meeting lobby, improving control over who/what joins meetings.

## 🤖 Copilot & AI in Teams
- **Agent Store governance expands:** Customers can now submit agents built in Agent Builder to the Agent Store under "Built by your org," with admins reviewing and approving submissions in the Microsoft 365 Admin Center — enabling orgs to share validated agents at scale while maintaining governance.
- **Copilot Cowork billing hard cutoff already in effect:** Organizations that used Cowork during Microsoft's Frontier preview had a grace period that ended July 1, 2026, after which any tenant without usage-based billing configured loses access to Cowork completely. Henrik should confirm this is set up if his org piloted Cowork.
- **AI content watermarking rolling out:** Watermarks for AI-generated content are now available for Microsoft 365 Copilot across Android, Windows, iOS, Mac and Web, controlled by an org policy setting for video and audio content altered by AI.

## 🔗 Ecosystem & Integrations
- **Teams Rooms Pro Management portal gets new BYOD reporting:** Admins can now proactively manage peripherals in BYOD rooms and desks via reports that flag if devices are faulty, undetectable, missing, or moved, with desk reports currently in public preview.
- **Partner licensing/pricing changes effective this month:** As of July 1, 2026, the Microsoft 365 Business with Copilot SKUs, previously previewed, are now generally available, and effective June 1, Microsoft introduced expanded license prerequisites for new Agent 365 purchases to ensure foundational security, identity, compliance, and management capabilities.
- **Front-of-room controls for town halls:** Teams Rooms on Windows now default to attendee view on the front-of-room display when invited as a presenter for town halls/webinars, while presenters retain full console control including green room and off-stage management.

## Meetingroom Equipment
- The main Teams Rooms certified hardware vendors remain Logitech, Poly, HP, Yealink, Neat, Crestron, Cisco, Lenovo, Jabra, Sennheiser and Shure.
- All-in-one touch boards running Windows are gaining Miracast support alongside Teams Cast and HDMI ingest, enabling cable-free screen mirroring for presentations.
- Teams Rooms on Windows now shows room health signal banners on both the front-of-room display and console when critical issues arise — this notification cannot be disabled.

## 🌐 Broader Collaboration Landscape
- **Slack:** Slack has made its Slackbot MCP client generally available, connecting apps and shared channels through one conversational workspace, with a partner ecosystem of 20+ apps including Salesforce, Canva, Linear and Zoom.
- **Webex:** Starting with version 46.7 released July 7, 2026, Cisco is ending support for iOS 17 and iPadOS 17 on the Webex App, with new users unable to download for those OS versions.
- **Zoom:** Zoom's AI Companion can now take notes across third-party meeting platforms including Google Meet, Microsoft Teams and Webex, not just Zoom meetings.

## ⚡ Action Items & Things to Watch
- Verify whether yesterday's (15 July) Teams disruption reports affected Henrik's tenant — check the Microsoft 365 admin health dashboard for any lingering intermittent issues.
- If the org piloted Copilot Cowork under Frontier, confirm usage-based billing is configured — access was cut off for unconfigured tenants after July 1.
- Track the Teams Rooms Android Gallery update and relocated Room Optimization Mode rollout (both mid-to-late July GA targets) — plan a validation pass on meeting room devices once they land.
