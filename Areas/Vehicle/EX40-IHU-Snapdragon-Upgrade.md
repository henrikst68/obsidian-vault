# Volvo EX40 (MY25) — IHU 4.0 → 4.2 Snapdragon Upgrade

## Background
- MY25 EX40 (and other AAOS-era Volvos, MY21–25) ship with **IHU 4.0**, using an Intel Atom processor for the infotainment unit.
- **MY26** cars get **IHU 4.2**, with a Qualcomm Snapdragon Cockpit Platform — Volvo claims >2x speed and up to 10x graphics performance vs. IHU 4.0.
- MY25 cars will get the new "Volvo Car UX" software via OTA, but full benefit requires the IHU 4.2 hardware.

## Status as of June 2026
- **No official Volvo-wide retrofit program yet**, but it is technically confirmed working:
  - IHU 4.2 (Snapdragon) is backwards-compatible with older AAOS cars (MY21–25) with IHU 4.0.
  - Volvo dealer Technical Journal (TJ37480.1.0, Aug 2025) confirms part supersession is possible: "If the IHU is replaced on a vehicle, the spare part may, in some cases, be superseded to the new IHU 4.2."
  - Counter-document (MC-11023544-0001) states there's officially "no Software Product available" for installing IHU 4.2 into MY21–25 vehicles — i.e. not formally supported, but evidently doable in practice.
- **No warranty claim accepted for this** — must be requested/paid out of pocket.
- Polestar (sister brand) already sells this as an official retrofit for Polestar 2 (~€940), but the Polestar part does NOT fit Volvo — different part number needed (Volvo part: 36003382).

## Real-world experience (US, March 2026)
- One owner successfully completed the swap: paid **$1,569 total out of pocket**, including a lifetime warranty on the IHU from Volvo.
- Others sourced the part for as low as **$750** if self-ordering.
- Process: dealer's service advisor needs to open a case (e.g. in TIE) explicitly stating it's **not a warranty replacement** and that the customer wants to pay out of pocket — warranty-route requests are far more stringent/likely rejected.
- Swap itself: hardware exchange (~3 hrs) + software reload via VIDA. New unit doesn't come pre-loaded with software; dealer or VIDA tool loads it. New unit arrives running the new Volvo Car UX out of the box.
- Feedback: "night and day" improvement in infotainment responsiveness/snappiness.
- All of the above is US-based — dealer behavior/process may differ in Denmark/EU.

## Action for Henrik
- Worth a casual inquiry next time the EX40 is in for service:
  - Reference part number **36003382** (IHU 4.2 Snapdragon) directly rather than asking generically for "an upgrade" (generic VIN lookups return no part for MY25).
  - Ask if they can open a non-warranty case to source/install the part, similar to the US experience.
  - Expect cost in the €700–1500 range depending on whether self-sourcing the part is possible/dealer cooperation.
- Keep an eye on whether Volvo announces an official retrofit program (rumored to be "seriously looking into it" as of late 2025).
