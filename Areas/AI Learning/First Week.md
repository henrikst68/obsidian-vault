# First Week

Five small actions, one per workday. Each is ~20–40 minutes. The goal isn't to learn AI in a week — it's to use every piece of this system once, so the habits stick.

---

## Monday — Read one concept note

Open [[Concepts/Token]]. Read it. Tick the checkbox in [[Roadmap]] Phase 1.

Then open OpenAI's tokenizer playground (platform.openai.com/tokenizer) and paste in:
- Your name and address (Danish)
- A paragraph of English
- One of your existing notes (e.g. [[Briefings/Collaboration Morning Brief 2026-05-07]])

Notice how different they tokenize. That tactile experience beats any explanation.

**Done when:** you've ticked one Roadmap box.

---

## Tuesday — Make your first Resource note

Pick **one** thing from the [[Reading Log]] queue. Anthropic's prompt engineering overview is the safest bet.

Spend 30 minutes on it. Then copy [[Templates/Resource Template]] into a new file under `Areas/AI Learning/` and fill it in. Even badly. Especially badly — the point is to use the template.

Add a row to the "Consumed" table in [[Reading Log]].

**Done when:** you have one Resource note that didn't exist yesterday.

---

## Wednesday — Write your own concept note

Pick a concept that came up in Tuesday's reading. Could be anything — *chain of thought*, *few-shot*, *function calling*, whatever stuck.

Copy [[Templates/Concept Template]] into `Areas/AI Learning/Concepts/Your Concept.md` and fill it in. The "Why it matters" section is the one that forces real understanding — don't skip it.

Link back from one of the existing concept notes ([[Concepts/Token]] etc.) so it's not orphaned.

**Done when:** there's a 5th note in your Concepts folder.

---

## Thursday — Start one experiment

You're a tinkerer — this is the day that should feel best.

Copy [[Templates/Experiment Template]] into `Areas/AI Learning/Experiments/` for something **small**. Suggestions:

- A prompt that turns one of your existing Briefings into a single-paragraph executive summary
- A prompt that takes a Home Assistant sensor reading and writes a one-line natural-language status
- A prompt that critiques one of your own notes

Don't try to ship it. Just: hypothesis, what you'll try, fill in the log entry for today.

**Done when:** there's at least one experiment with status = `planning` and a hypothesis written.

---

## Friday — First weekly review

Open the [[README|AI Learning README]]. Run the weekly review checklist at the bottom. Spend exactly 15 minutes — set a timer.

Fill in "Current focus" for next week. One sentence.

**Done when:** the review checklist is ticked through and "Current focus" is filled in.

---

## End of week

If you did all five: you've used the index, the roadmap, two templates, the reading log, made notes in two folders, and run a review. The system has been *used*, not just *built*. That's the whole point.

If you only did two or three: that's also fine. Tune the rest down. A system you sometimes use beats a perfect one you abandon.
